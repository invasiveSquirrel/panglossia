# Dutch Vocabulary

## Essential Verbs
- **zijn** (to be)
  - IPA: [zɛɪ̯n]
  - Gender: N/A
  - Part of Speech: Verb
  - Conjugations: ben (I am), bent (you are), is (he/she is)
  - Example: "Ik ben een leraar" (I am a teacher)

- **hebben** (to have)
  - IPA: [ˈhɛbə(n)]
  - Gender: N/A
  - Part of Speech: Verb
  - Conjugations: heb (I have), hebt (you have), heeft (he/she has)
  - Example: "Ik heb een boek" (I have a book)

- **doen** (to do)
  - IPA: [dun]
  - Gender: N/A
  - Part of Speech: Verb
  - Conjugations: doe (I do), doet (you do), doet (he/she does)
  - Example: "Wat doe jij?" (What do you do?)

- **gaan** (to go)
  - IPA: [ɡaːn]
  - Gender: N/A
  - Part of Speech: Verb
  - Conjugations: ga (I go), gaat (you go), gaat (he/she goes)
  - Example: "Ik ga naar de markt" (I go to the market)

- **komen** (to come)
  - IPA: [ˈkoːmə(n)]
  - Gender: N/A
  - Part of Speech: Verb
  - Conjugations: kom (I come), komt (you come), komt (he/she comes)
  - Example: "Hij komt morgen" (He comes tomorrow)
